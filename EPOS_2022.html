The characteristic fragmentation scale


The Jeans Mass MJ is the mass where gravity and thermal pressure are balanced.

MJ ∝ T3/2 ρ−1/2
  • Higher density means greater gravity, leading to collapse and fragmentation
  • Higher temperature means greater pressure, providing support against gravitational collapse and suppressing it.

The characteristic fragmentation scale is larger

Jeans Mass MJ ∝ T3/2 ρ−1/2
Ginsburg+ 2017
  • (describe plot)
  • In the warm neighborhood of accreting massive stars, the Jeans mass is higher
  • Despite the high density, the Jeans mass is 5-10 times greater than locally

What governs the star formation rate?

Kennicutt & Evans 2012
  • Kennicutt-Schmidt relation ties gas surface density and stellar surface density
  • Good correlation over several orders of magnitude, but the scatter is substantial and important
  • The timescales are 100 Myr to 10 Gyr and show the "depletion scales", the time for the gas to fully convert to stars
